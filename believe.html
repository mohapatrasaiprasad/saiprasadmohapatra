  2. Believe Me

Recently, I visited a store to buy some electronic appliances. Looking at me, baffled, the salesperson found the opportunity for himself to showcase his talent. Maintaining the social distancing, he stood few centimeters apart and asked, “Sir! What are you looking for ?”. “Washing Machine,” I replied unconsciously.

“We have the best products in the market.” And he went on to explain the features and showed me every model like a guide explaining everything to the tourists visiting a museum. I was now more confused. I never knew technological advancement had reached such a peak we can directly heat the water inside. I took a seat, thought for a moment, and finalized a product.

“Sir, are you sure?” “Of course, I am. You just explained the features, and you sound very positive about this product.” He smirked and said, “ Yes, it’s the best. However, I would suggest you choose front-loading instead of top-loading. And Believe me, you won’t regret it.”

Should I believe him, or Should I be skeptical about his words? How do I know whatever he is saying is for the benefit of both the parties or is he only concerned about his profit margin and commission. And how the belief system works?

“ Belief” is an invisible force. It is the guiding principle we follow for decision-making. What we believe is interconnected to everything we experience. If one experiences changes, the whole belief system gets affected. The goal of this system is for human survival, Passing the genes, Enriching mental health, and Maintaining social status.

Like the problem I faced in the electronic store, when people encounter different viewpoints, their belief system gets triggered. However, most people stick to their perceptions and belief. Own beliefs are hard to change over a short period. But prolonged exposure to a particular view may change your perspective.

“Man is what he believes” – Anton Chekhov. Your belief defines you. Again it’s not static. It’s us who make a rational choice. Our belief is the reason for the reality we are facing now. It is the reason for a range of decisions we make in our lives, from moral choices to the practice of religion or choosing a political party. Mental representation is the building block of your conscious thought

The emotional brain is no longer confined to classical locales of the hippocampus, amygdala, hypothalamus. Our frontal lobes are responsible for what conscious awareness is entering our minds. Our belief system is responsible for what portion of that conscious awareness will be consumed at the end. Are those constant? Belief can’t change?? Our cell membranes are flexible, so we can say our belief is subject to change. It can be categorized as placebo belief which is your positive, and Nocebo belief which is more negative.

However, there are some real dangers here:
1. people who think that something is wrong with them to start trying to ‘get rid of these negative feelings. They don’t give themselves the space to process the new information and integrate it into their existing belief system. As a result, they develop a long-term identity crisis.
2. people who see the new information and new beliefs as a threat will often continue to reject it altogether. In doing so, they do not open up space for themselves to absorb this new information and therefore continue to have strong, negative feelings for an extended period.

What is the solution for this? Basically, it is to let go and accept this period of confusion and anxiety for what it is.

Three vital elements of belief systems
Homeostasis: belief systems constantly strive towards reaching a state of equilibrium—a form of relative unity.
Self-regulation: belief systems can adapt themselves to the external conditions in which they are placed. In that sense, the system can reach a state of unity regardless of the circumstances through a constant feedback mechanism between internal beliefs and external factors.
Autopoiesis: belief systems are capable of reproducing and maintaining themselves. It can change quite significantly and become significantly more complex as part of this process.

So, we should understand one of the most fundamental processes that enable humans to be human – the process of believing and take cognitive and emotional perspectives for personal perspective making.


I bought the washing machine from a different store.
